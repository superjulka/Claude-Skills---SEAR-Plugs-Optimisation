> **Sanitised public version.** Replace `[BRACKETED]` values with your own. Internal pricing, contact details and file paths have been removed; the original runs for searplugs.com across UK + DE/ES/FR/PT/NL.

You are running the weekly backlink research task for `[YOUR-SITE]`. The site owner reviews and sends all outreach personally — **never send emails automatically; drafts only.**

### STEP 0 — Load the existing tracker

Read `[TRACKER-PATH]/backlink-opportunities.xlsx`. Use the **Pipeline** tab to de-duplicate new finds; note the next empty **Weekly Log** row.

### STEP 1 — Run all 8 strategies via web search

Collect max **6 new opportunities per strategy per run**, prioritising relevance. For each: URL, domain, page title, market, strategy type, contact email if visible, notes. Run home-market and priority-market (local-language) query variants for strategies 1, 2, 3, 4 and 7.

1. **Broken link building** *(highest priority — target ≥5 confirmed broken links per run)*. Search for gear guides, "best of" articles and prevention resources in your niche; fetch candidate pages; extract outbound links; check each with a status request. Only log **confirmed** 404/410s — those dead links are what you pitch your content as the replacement for.
2. **Resource pages** — "best [product category]", kit lists and buyer's guides that don't mention your brand (`-yourdomain.com` in queries). Fetch each page to confirm the gap before logging.
3. **Unlinked brand mentions** — pages that mention your brand in plain text without a link. Easiest conversions; verify the mention is really about you and genuinely unlinked.
4. **Competitor link gap** — reviews and roundups covering your category that omit you. Never name competitors in the eventual outreach; pitch on your own merits.
5. **Digital PR / journalist requests** — live expert-source queries on your topics (Connectively, Featured.com and search). Log deadlines; note seasonal hooks.
6. **Podcasts & YouTube** — shows covering your niche's gear, health or training topics, with recent relevant episodes noted.
7. **Professional & partner listings** — for us: ENT clinics/audiologists with patient-resource pages, surf schools' kit lists, swim clubs' recommended equipment. Sub-type in notes.
8. **Guest posts** — publications with "write for us" pages in your niche; log contributor-guideline URLs.

### STEP 2 — Score

Priority 3 (high): unlinked mentions, live PR queries with deadlines, confirmed broken links on strong pages. Priority 2: resource pages, competitor gaps, podcasts, professional listings. Priority 1: guest posts, unconfirmed-fit prospects. Sort highest first.

### STEP 3 — Append to the Pipeline tab

One row per find: date, URL, domain, title, market, strategy type, contact name/email, Status="New", notes. **Never modify existing rows.**

### STEP 4 — Draft outreach for the top 5

Create Gmail **drafts** (never send). If no contact email was found, use your own address as the recipient placeholder with `[ACTION: find & replace recipient]` at the top of the body. Every draft starts with `[REVIEW BEFORE SENDING — auto-drafted by weekly backlink task]`. Set those rows to Status="Drafted".

**Outreach rules that earned their place:**
- **Always hyperlink the exact article you're referencing** — never mention a page without linking it, and never link just the homepage.
- For publications and review sites, lead with a **commercial partnership hook** (reader discount + `[YOUR AFFILIATE TERMS]`) — not "can we send you a free product". This replaced our v1 approach after real response data.
- For professional/partner targets, use the recommendation angle relevant to their audience; reference their specific resource page.
- For broken-link targets, name the exact dead URL you found.

### STEP 5 — Update the Weekly Log

Counts per strategy, drafts created, and a one-line summary. Leave the "sent" and "won" columns for the human — they're filled manually.

### STEP 6 — Won-link monitoring

For Won Links rows last checked >21 days ago: re-fetch, confirm the link is still present, update status (Live/Broken/Removed), flag removals for follow-up.

### STEP 7 — Summary email draft

One plain-text Gmail draft to `[YOUR-EMAIL]`: results at a glance, top priorities by category, a ready-to-send action list, and notes (deadlines, seasonal hooks, anything needing careful review).

### CONSTRAINTS

- Drafts only — a human sends everything
- **Geographic filter:** only target markets you can actually serve (we can't ship to the US, so US publications are never logged)
- Never name competitor brands in outreach
- Respect your brand's banned-terminology list in every draft
- Flag anything touching medical/health claims for careful human review
