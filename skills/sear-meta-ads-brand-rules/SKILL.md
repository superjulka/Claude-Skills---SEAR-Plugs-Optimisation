# SEAR Plugs — Meta Ads Brand Rules

Use this skill as the guardrail layer for all SEAR Meta ad copy. It contains everything
you need to write on-brand, compliant, effective ads — and to catch violations before
they go live.

## Non-Negotiables

These rules are absolute. Violate any of them and the copy fails the review — no exceptions.

### 1. Custom language policy

| ❌ NEVER use | ✅ USE instead |
|---|---|
| "custom moulded" | "find your fit" |
| "custom-moulded" | "a fit tailored to your ear" |
| "bespoke" | "sized to your ear" |
| "made-to-measure" | "3 tips + 4 wings for your exact fit" |
| "made-to-fit" | "find your fit" |

**"Custom fit" is NOT used either** — even as a description of the sizing experience, it
risks implying manufacturing customisation (moulding, casting, bespoke construction).
Use "find your fit" or "sized to your ear" instead. This aligns the ad rules with the
site-wide project instructions, which ban all "custom" variants outright.

### 2. No competitor names

Never mention any competitor brand by name in ad copy,
overlays, captions, or CTAs. Differentiate using product facts ("blocks water at 9dB,
not 15dB") — never by naming competitors.

### 3. Medical claims boundary

**OK to say:**
- "helps prevent surfer's ear"
- "cold water can cause bone growth in the ear canal — SEAR blocks it"
- "protects your ears from cold water and wind"
- "surfer's ear prevention"

**Never say:**
- "clinically proven", "medically approved", "FDA approved"
- "cures surfer's ear" or "treats surfer's ear"
- "prevents all ear infections"
- Any specific prognosis, dosage, or clinical claim

### 4. No inflated claims

Only claim what the product demonstrably does. Superlatives ("the best", "the world's
only") need substantiation. Use specific, credible facts instead: "9dB acoustic filter",
"medical-grade silicone", "stays in through wipeouts".

## Brand Voice

**In one sentence:** Write like a surfer who genuinely cares about ear health — expert,
warm, direct. Not salesy. Earn trust, then let the product speak.

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| Active voice | Passive voice |
| Sentences under 20 words | Long, winding sentences |
| Specific facts | Vague superlatives |
| "Blocks cold water" | "High quality protection" |
| "9dB filter keeps you aware" | "State of the art technology" |
| Warm and direct | Hyped or pushy |

**Filler phrases to cut on sight:** "In today's world", "It's important to note",
"High quality", "State of the art", "At the end of the day", "World class".

## The Core Tension (use it)

Every strong SEAR ad plays on the same tension: **you need to protect your ears, but
you don't want to be cut off from the ocean.** Most plugs solve the first problem but
create the second. SEAR solves both.

Build copy around this before reaching for anything else. It's the reason people buy.

**Examples of the tension in action:**
- "Block water. Not the sound of the break."
- "Protect your ears without losing the ocean."
- "Still hear your friends. Still hear the surf. Block the cold."
- "9dB blocks water. 15dB blocks everything. You know which one we made."

## Three Approved Angles

These map to the three creative angles in the SEAR Meta Ads Creative Brief.

**Angle 1 — Tension Hook**
Lead with the 9dB vs 15dB differentiator. The awareness angle. Best for cold prospecting
audiences who surf or swim. Primary hook: "Block Water. Embrace Sound."

**Angle 2 — Consequence**
Lead with surfer's ear as the pain point. Cold water, bone growth, eventual surgery.
Prevention framing. Best for surfers 28+ who've heard of surfer's ear but aren't yet
protecting themselves. Primary hook: "Cold water is slowly changing your ears."

**Angle 3 — Product Demo**
Lead with the sizing system. 3 tips + 4 wings = a secure fit without moulding. Credibility
and specificity. Best for retargeting (people who visited the site) and audiences who've
tried other plugs. Primary hook: "3 tips. 4 wings. One watertight fit."

## EU Market Guidance

When targeting EU audiences (FR, DE, NL, PT, ES) or running UK ads with EU expansion:

- **Always show EUR price alongside GBP:** "£39.95 / €45.95" — don't make EU customers
  do the conversion.
- **Reference EU surf/swim locations where natural:** Hossegor (FR), Peniche/Nazaré (PT),
  Mundaka/Zarautz (ES), Scheveningen (NL), Lacanau (FR), Sardinia (IT).
- **Don't force EU references** if the post is UK-specific or the location doesn't fit.
  Relevance is the test.

## Copy Review Checklist

Before approving any ad copy, run through this list:

- [ ] No "custom moulded", "bespoke", "made-to-measure", or "made-to-fit"
- [ ] No "custom" variants anywhere — "find your fit" / "sized to your ear" used instead
- [ ] No competitor brand names
- [ ] No unsubstantiated medical claims ("cures", "clinically proven", "FDA approved")
- [ ] Active voice throughout
- [ ] No filler phrases
- [ ] Product differentiated on facts (9dB, medical-grade silicone, wing system)
- [ ] Headline ≤ 40 characters (Meta limit)
- [ ] Primary text ≤ 125 characters for feed; longer OK for Reels
- [ ] If EU-targeted: EUR price included alongside GBP
- [ ] CTA button wording matches ad message (Shop Now / Protect Your Ears / Find Your Fit)

## Quick Copy Examples

**Good headline:** "Block Water. Embrace Sound." ✅
**Bad headline:** "Custom moulded earplugs for surfers" ❌

**Good primary text:**
> Surf earplugs with a 9dB acoustic filter — block cold water without blocking sound.
> Medical-grade silicone. Stays in through wipeouts. £39.95 → searplugs.com ✅

**Bad primary text:**
> Our clinically proven, bespoke surf earplugs are the best on the market. ❌
> (breaks medical claim rule, uses "bespoke", uses unsubstantiated superlative)

**Good retargeting:**
> Still thinking about it? A watertight fit without the mould — from £39.95. ✅

**Bad retargeting:**
> Our custom moulded earplugs are waiting for you. ❌

## Working with /ad-copy-variant-generator

When using the variant generator for SEAR Meta ads:

1. Pass performance data as normal.
2. After variants are generated, run every variant through the Copy Review Checklist above.
3. If any variant fails a rule, rewrite that variant before presenting to the user — don't
   present non-compliant copy.
4. Label each variant with its angle (Tension / Consequence / Demo) so testing is clean.
